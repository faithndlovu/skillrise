Our Story
A small idea that grew into a movement

SkillRise started with one person, one skill, and a belief that young people in our communities deserved more than unemployment — they deserved a real chance.

Where it all began

The initiative started informally — a few young people gathering to learn boma sewing in a backyard. Word spread quickly. More youth came. Then parents started asking how their children could join. Within months, what began as an informal gathering had become a structured programme training dozens of young people every month.

Today, SkillRise offers over 8 vocational programmes, has reached multiple communities, and has helped hundreds of young people start earning real income. But the heart of it remains the same: practical skills, community support, and the belief that potential just needs the right environment to flourish.

We're growing — and we're just getting started.

Every young person has something to offer. Our job is just to give them the tools to show the world what that is.
— SkillRise Founder
